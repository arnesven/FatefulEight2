## Trouble in Lower Theln
A simple adventure for a young group.

### The Hook: The Missing Moon‑Pies
It’s a bright, breezy morning in the coastal town of Lower Theln. The heroes (your wife and daughter) are enjoying the salty air when suddenly—

“Oh nooooo!”

A cry echoes across the docks. It’s Harold, the cheerful halfling baker. His famous Glazed Moon‑Pies, packed in a big wooden crate for the festival in Upper Theln, have vanished! The festival boat leaves in less than an hour.

Harold is too distraught to investigate, so the heroes step in.

### A Busy Day in Lower Theln

Today isn’t just any day—several small events are happening around town:

* The Seagull Parade: Local children are tossing crumbs to attract as many seagulls as possible. It’s loud, silly, and chaotic.

* The Shell‑Polishing Contest: A few elderly townsfolk are proudly showing off their shiniest seashells.

* The Fisherman’s Grumble: Old Captain Merrin is loudly complaining that someone borrowed his favorite fishing hat.

* The Mini‑Market: A few stalls are selling trinkets, tiny pastries, and colorful ribbons.

These details help the town feel alive and give the heroes fun things to notice.

### Clues on the Docks

A quick search (Collaborative Search 4) reveals:
* A trail of sticky blue glaze (Harold’s secret ingredient).
* Tiny, webbed footprints, still muddy and damp.
* The tracks skip the road south and instead head east, toward the rolling green hills by the Thelnius River.

### The Journey East

The trail winds out of town and into sunny, grassy hills. Birds chirp. The river sparkles. Everything feels peaceful… until the trail reaches a rushing little stream.

### Obstacle: The Slippery Stream

The stepping stones are covered in moss and wobble underfoot.

Possible solutions:
* Find a fallen log (Collaborative Search 6)
* Use a rope (Collective Acrobatics 5, failure causes falling in the water, see "wading" option below)
* Jump across from a rocky outcropping (Collective Acrobatics 7, failure causes injury: 2 damage)
* Just wading across, but the water is cold and there is current. Each character which wades across loses one Stamina Point.
* Invent something clever

Encourage creativity.

### A Strange Sound…

On the far side of the stream, the heroes hear:

“Crunch… crunch… smack… YUM!”

Something is very happily eating.

The Frogmen’s Picnic

In a cozy hollow in the hillside, the heroes find the culprits:

Three Frogmen — small, silly, frog‑like creatures who aren’t mean, just greedy and messy.

They’ve cracked open the crate and are absolutely coated in blue glaze. They’re having the best picnic of their lives.

#### Solving the Problem (No Combat Needed)

Offer gentle, playful options:

* Distract Them – Offer snacks or something shiny to trade for the remaining pies.
* Scare Them (Playfully) – A big “BOO!” or banging pots might send them hopping away.
* Tickle Fight – The frogmen are helpless against tickles and giggles.

### The Resolution: Pastry Protectors!

The frogmen scamper off, leaving the crate behind. They ate a few pies, but most are still safe.
The heroes hurry back to the docks just as the festival boat blows its horn. Harold is overjoyed and declares them:

“Protectors of the Pastry!”

He rewards them with:

* A pouch of shiny silver coins
* Golden badges (you can draw them)
* A giant Moon‑Pie to share

### Frogman Filchers: Stats
Health Points: 3
Speed: 6
Damage: 1

A Frogman Filcher will retreat at the end of any round in which it took damage.
