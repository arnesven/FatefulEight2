# Adventure 3: The Midsummer Melon Meltdown

## Adventure Overview

* The Setup: A two-day riverboat journey to Upper Theln, where the heroes meet a friendly passenger and spot a suspicious gang.
* The Investigation: Exploring a vibrant festival town, meeting the local mayor and merchants, and uncovering a plot to ruin the big day.
* The Climax: A festive showdown behind the main stage where two bumbling brothers change sides, a giant melon explosion creates a distraction, and a dangerous new rival escapes.

## Part 1: The Riverboat to Upper Theln
The adventure begins aboard The River Mist, a cozy trading boat heading upriver. 
The journey takes two days, giving the PCs plenty of time to look around.

### The Friendly Passenger
Sharing the deck with the heroes is Gideon Lightroot, an elderly, eccentric human 
herbalist travelling to the festival to sell dried river-herbs. He is incredibly 
chatty and happily fills the PCs in on what to expect.

He explains that Upper Theln is celebrating the Annual Midsummer Melon Festival, 
famous for its "Glow-Melons"—giant, magically bio-luminescent watermelons that 
taste like honey and light up like lanterns.

He tells them the grand finale of the festival is the cutting of the Grand 
Emperor Melon, the largest and brightest melon grown that year, which is kept 
behind the town's main stage.

### The Suspicious Crew
Also on board is a rough-looking gang keeping to themselves on the lower deck.

The Leader: Hilda "The Anvil" Stonebreaker. A muscular, scowling dwarf with a 
shaved head except for a bright red braided topknot. She wears heavy studded 
leather and spends her time aggressively sharpening a dagger.

The Thugs: Three shifty-looking minions—two brawny, scowling Half-Orcs named 
Grim and Gore, and one twitchy Halfling named Snicker who wears a hood pulled low.

The Brothers: Jaq and Jimmy. Two human brothers wearing matching, 
slightly-too-large leather vests. Jaq is tall and constantly nervous, and lugs a pick-axe around. 
While Jimmy is shorter, chubby, and currently trying (and failing) to juggle river 
pebbles to pass the time. Jimmy wields a light crossbow.

The Clue: During the trip, the players might notice (Perception 5) Hilda looking 
over a crude map of Upper Theln's town square. If the Witch sneaks close, she 
overhears Hilda snapping quietly to the brothers: 

<i><center>"You two morons better not 
botch the distraction. When the lights go out, we strike the target and get 
out."</i></center>

It's clear they are planning something bad, but they haven't explicitly 
said what yet.

## Part 2: Welcome to Upper Theln!
Upon arrival, the town is a whirlwind of color. Green and pink banners hang everywhere, and the smell of sweet melon pies fills the air. The town is bustling as everyone prepares for tomorrow's big event.

### Colorful Local NPCs
As the PCs explore the town the day before the festival, they meet:

<b>Mayor Rutherford</b>: A suave, impeccably dressed Dark Elf with silver hair. He is 
incredibly proud of Upper Theln but overly stressed about the festival. When 
he welcomes the PCs (and thanks them if they mention saving the pastries from 
Lower Theln!), he accidentally lets a secret slip:

<i><center>"Everything must be perfect! With all the tourists here, my 
office treasury vault is absolutely overflowing with gold coin taxes. If anything 
happened to it... oh, but listen to me worry! Welcome to our town!"</center></i>

<b>Barnaby Bumble</b>: A jovial, elderly human wizard who wears a hat shaped like a 
melon slice. He is the "Grand Melon-Mancer," responsible for using magic to
make the Grand Emperor Melon extra sparkly for the finale.

<b>Penelope "Penny" Paws</b>: A friendly Wood-Elf merchant running a ring-toss game stall. 
She is a major gossip and mentions seeing "a scary dwarf woman and three ugly 
thugs" asking weird questions about the back entrance to the Mayor's town hall.

Meeting Jaq and Jimmy
While wandering the market, the PCs bump into Jaq and Jimmy, who are arguing 
near a food stall. Jimmy accidentally drops a pouch. When the PCs return it, 
the brothers are incredibly grateful.

Jaq admits they are just looking for honest work but "got mixed up with a 
really mean boss."

Jimmy complains that Hilda doesn't let them eat any festival food. If the PCs 
buy them a slice of melon pie, they instantly win the brothers' affection.

## Part 3: The Day of the Festival & The Proof
The next day, the festival is in full swing! There are games, music, and the 
saved pastries from the first adventure are a huge hit. However, danger looms.

### Connecting the Dots
While enjoying the festivities, the Witch and Forester spot Snicker 
(the twitchy halfling thug) sneaking into Barnaby Bumble’s magic workshop 
directly behind the main stage.

If they investigate or corner him, they discover:
* A jar of Alchemical Smoke Powder meant to be stuffed inside the Grand Emperor Melon.
* A written checklist in Hilda's handwriting:
```
1. Rig the big melon. 
2. Melon explodes, creates smoke panic. 
3. Rob the Dark Elf's treasury vault while everyone runs.
```

Remembering Mayor Rutherford's accidental slip-up about his overflowing treasury 
vault, the PCs now have definitive proof of the heist!

## Part 4: The Melon Mash Showdown!
The heroes confront Hilda and the rest of her gang just behind the main stage. 
Out front, Mayor Rutherford is at the podium making his big festival speech to 
the crowd, completely unaware that right behind the curtain sits the rigged, giant 
Grand Emperor Melon.

### The Battle Lines
Hilda realizes the jig is up. "Get 'em, boys!" she yells.

The two Half-Orcs draw their clubs. But when she orders Jaq and Jimmy to attack, the brothers look at each other, remember the kindness (and the pie) the PCs gave them, and step in front of the Witch and Forester!

Jaq: <i>"No way, Hilda! These guys are nice! We're quitting!"</i>

Jimmy: <i>"Yeah! And your health plan sucks!"</i>

### The Twist & The Escape
A brief, fun combat begins. The players, Jaq, and Jimmy outmatch the two Half-Orcs 
and Snicker. However, just as the heroes corner Hilda, she swings her massive 
warhammer into the giant Grand Emperor Melon behind the stage!

The Distraction: The melon, packed with the alchemical smoke powder, explodes in 
a massive, spectacular shower of pink, glowing, sticky melon goo and thick green 
smoke!

The crowd out front panics, screaming about a "melon monster." By the time the 
smoke clears, Hilda the Anvil has escaped into the crowded alleys. However, her 
three minions (the two Half-Orcs and the Halfling) are pinned down under chunks 
of giant melon, completely captured.

## Conclusion: Rewards and New Friends
Mayor Rutherford arrives with the town guard, scraping glowing melon pulp off 
his fine silk robes. He is incredibly grateful to the PCs for protecting the 
town treasury.

### The Reward
The Mayor awards the Witch and Forester the "Golden Melon Medallion" 
(a shiny local medal of honor) and a pouch of gold.

### The Verdict
Jaq and Jimmy spot the town guard and nervously step forward. Thanks to 
the PCs vouching for their help in the fight, Mayor Rutherford lets them 
off with a strict warning and some community service (cleaning up the sticky 
melon explosion).

### New Alliance
Covered in pink goo, Jaq and Jimmy approach the players. Jaq says:

<i><center> “You guys are real heroes. Hilda is going to be looking 
for revenge, and she's bad news. Can we come with you? We can carry your bags,
cook, and... well, Jimmy can almost juggle!”</i></center>

Jaq is a level 2 Human Miner and Jimmy is a level 2 Human Spy
