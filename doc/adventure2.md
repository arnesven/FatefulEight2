# Adventure 2: The Curse of the Croaking Prince
A light, magical adventure beginning in the town of Lower Theln and leading into the mysterious Whispering Fens. This version includes subtle hints of the deeper enchantment tied to the ancient Lich Queen Niëlenna.

---

## Background: After the Moon-Pie Heist
The heroes return to Lower Theln after chasing away three mischievous frogmen who attempted to steal Harold the baker’s Moon-Pies. In their haste to escape, one frogman dropped a dagger unlike anything the townsfolk have ever seen. The blade is elegant, silver, and faintly glowing, its runes swirling like moonlit water.

When the Witch touches it, she feels a faint tug—as if someone far away whispered her name. The Forester notices that a few nearby flowers droop slightly when the dagger is held too close, only to perk up again when it’s moved away. These oddities are subtle enough to ignore, but they linger in the air like a half-remembered dream.

Word spreads quickly: the heroes found something strange.

---

## Asking Around Lower Theln
The characters can ask around town about the dagger (Collaborative Seek Info 6). Lower Theln is a friendly place, and everyone has an opinion.

Old Bram, the guard captain, squints at the dagger and mutters that frogmen have never carried anything fancier than a stick. The gossiping fisherwomen whisper that the runes look elven. Lady Tamsin, the mayor, urges the heroes to consult the town’s eccentric expert on ancient objects.

All paths lead to Master Quillberry.

---

## Master Quillberry’s Antiquarian Curiosities & Arcane Oddments
Master Quillberry’s shop is a tall, narrow building squeezed between two warehouses. Its windows are cluttered with dusty trinkets, glowing crystals, and a stuffed owl wearing spectacles. Inside, the air smells of old books and lavender polish.

Master Quillberry himself is a tiny gnome with hair like a startled dandelion and a magnifying glass permanently in hand. He is delighted by the dagger and examines it with theatrical enthusiasm.

He explains that the dagger is unmistakably elven, crafted with the refined magic of the Silver Court. But as he studies it further, his brow furrows. He taps the blade, and the resulting *ping* is slightly discordant.

He remarks that the dagger carries a second, older enchantment beneath the obvious one—something ancient, something he cannot identify. He laughs it off as a harmless oddity, but the moment lingers.

Quillberry directs the heroes to Lord Vaelith, a visiting High Elf scholar staying at the Moonbridge Inn.

---

## Lord Vaelith at the Moonbridge Inn
The Moonbridge Inn is warm and inviting, its lanterns glowing with soft amber light. Lord Vaelith sits alone at a corner table, sipping shimmering tea. He is tall, elegant, and dressed in silver and violet. His expression is calm—until he sees the dagger.

He recoils ever so slightly (Reactive Perception 5), his hand trembling when he reaches for it. Under his breath, he mutters a phrase in Elvish that the heroes do not understand, something like *“Her shadow lingers…”*

When questioned, he tries to remain composed, but he is a terrible liar. With gentle coaxing (Solo Persuade 6), he eventually confesses.

He explains that the dagger belongs to Prince Aeron of the Silver Court. In a moment of jealousy, Vaelith attempted a harmless prank spell that backfired, transforming Aeron into a giant frogman. Aeron fled into the Whispering Fens, and Vaelith has been hiding in shame ever since.

He also admits, reluctantly, that the dagger carries a legacy older than Aeron’s lineage—an enchantment tied to an ancestor the Silver Court does not speak of. He refuses to elaborate, insisting that some histories are better left buried.

Still, he urges the heroes to rescue Aeron and explains that the dagger is magically bonded to him. If the Witch channels magic through it, the blade will guide them.

When the Witch casts a spell on the dagger (Solo Magic Any 7), the runes glow brighter and the blade points like a compass needle. The Witch feels a momentary drain, as though a tiny bit of her energy has been pulled away. The glow has a faint violet undertone, colder than before.

The dagger emits a soft croaking chime, pointing east toward the Whispering Fens.

---

## Into the Whispering Fens
The Whispering Fens are quiet in that strange way marshes can be—buzzing insects, distant frog calls, and the soft *glorp* of mud underfoot. Wisps of pale mist curl around reeds. Every now and then, the dagger gives a faint, elegant *ping*.

The Forester’s skills shine here as she guides the party through treacherous terrain (Solo Survival 7). She notices webbed footprints, patches of quicksand disguised as normal mud, and slippery fallen logs that serve as natural bridges. Marsh-cranes explode into the air if startled, threatening to alert frogman scouts.

The Witch may hear a faint whisper when she holds the dagger alone—a woman’s voice, ancient and sad, saying something like *“Return…”* or *“Remember…”* The sound is so soft it could be imagination.

---

## The Frogman Village
The frogmen live in a cluster of reed huts built on stilts above the marsh water. Everything is decorated with bright flowers, shiny pebbles, dragonfly wings, and lumpy mud sculptures of frogs. The frogmen themselves are silly, curious, and easily distracted.

At the center of the village stands a glittering bamboo-and-gold cage. Inside sits a giant, rainbow-colored frogman wearing a crooked crown of reeds. He looks utterly miserable and is trying to spit out a dragonfly wing stuck to his tongue.

When he sees the heroes, he croaks urgently and gestures for them to come closer.

Up close, the frogman whispers in a deep, froggy voice that he is Prince Aeron of the Silver Court and begs for help. He explains that he was cursed by Lord Vaelith and fled into the marsh, only to be captured by frogmen who declared him their “Croaking Prince.”

When he sees the dagger, he croaks, “You brought… *her* dagger?” before quickly correcting himself. He is embarrassed—not because he knows the full truth, but because the Silver Court treats the ancient Lich Queen Niëlenna as a taboo ancestor.

---

## The Rescue Options
The heroes can choose how to free Aeron:

- **Sneaky Escape:** Distract the frogmen with noises (Solo Entertain 7) or illusions (Solo Blue Magic 7), pick the lock (Solo Security 5) or break the cage (Collaborative Labor 7), and tiptoe away.
- **The Great Bargain:** Offer the frogmen something they want more than a king, such as a giant frog statue (Collaborative Labor 6, 5 Materials), a Moon-Pie tribute (character will need to go back to Lower Theln to get some), or a new ritual song (Collaborative Entertain 8).

---

## Breaking the Curse
Once safely away, the Witch can attempt to break the spell. This is done either by casting the "Dispel" spell, or by succeeding in a Collaborative Blue Magic 6 skill check. 

When the spell is broken, the dagger glows brilliantly, runes swirling into the air. For a split second, the Witch sees a ghostly silhouette of an elven queen behind Aeron—regal, sorrowful, and watching. Then it vanishes.

With a soft *pop*, Aeron transforms back into a tall, elegant High Elf. He bows deeply and thanks the heroes.

He gifts the Witch a silver frog-shaped charm that glows faintly when danger is near and occasionally croaks when hungry for magic.

Aeron promises a future invitation to the Silver Court, a feast in the heroes’ honor, and a song sung by elven bards about the Hero of the Whispering Fens. Later, a frogman messenger may arrive in Lower Theln with a basket of Moon-Pies as a thank-you.

The dagger remains with the heroes for now—its glow soft, its whispers faint, its secrets waiting for the right moment to awaken.
